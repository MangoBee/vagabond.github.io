Playing Sorcerer

The sorcerer is a squishy blaster focused on Casting Spells for instant burst damage. You should stay in safety to Cast.

Class Features

Glamour

Sorcerer: 1st Level

You can Cast Spells using Influence.

Spells: You learn 4 Spells. You learn 1 other Spell every 2 Sorcerer Levels hereafter.

Mana: Your Maximum Mana is equal to (4 √ó your Sorcerer Level), and the highest amount of Mana you can spend to Cast a Spell is equal to (Presence + half your Sorcerer Level, round up). You regain spent Mana when you Rest.

Tap

Sorcerer: 1st Level

You gain the Metamagic Perk. Further, when you Cast, you can reduce your Max HP to regain Mana equal to (2 √ó the reduction). This reduction ends when you Rest. If you die from this reduction, the Cast resolves before your death, and your body is vaporized.

Spell-Slinger

Sorcerer: 2nd Level

You Crit on Cast Checks on a roll of 19 to 20, and your Spells use a d8 damage die, rather than a d6.

Quickening

Sorcerer: 4th Level

You can skip your Move to Cast a Spell. No Mana can be spent on this Casting.

Arcane Anomaly

Sorcerer: 6th Level

You reduce Magic damage you take by half.

Spell Twinning

Sorcerer: 8th Level

If you Cast the same Spell twice on a Turn, the second Cast Check is Favored.

Overpowered

Sorcerer: 10th Level

You Crit on Cast Checks on a roll of 18 to 20. Further, when you Cast, you can choose to gain 2 Fatigue. If you do, you regain Cd6 Mana at the start of your Turns, and you can spend as much Mana as you like to Cast.
