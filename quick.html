Ragged Hollow
Vagabond
Quick Rules
Vagabond is a tabletop roleplaying game built off the foundations of DnD B/X, Pathfinder, Dragonbane, Errant, Knave, Cairn, Into the Odd, Shadowdark, Shadow of the Demon Lord, and other OSR-style games.

Checks

To make a Check, roll a d20 and compare it to the Difficulty:

  • Crit: d20 shows 20.
  • Pass: d20 ≥ Difficulty.
  • Fail: d20 < Difficulty.

When you Crit on an Attack, Cast, or a Save:

  • Attack / Cast: Deal extra damage equal to the Stat used.
  • Save: You ignore the effect and can still take an Action.

Favor & Hinder

Circumstances can make a Check easier or harder:

  • Favored: Roll d20 + d6 (bonus).
  • Hindered: Roll d20 − d6 (penalty).
  • Favor and Hinder cancel 1-for-1; you can only be one or the other.

Combat

Combat is split into Rounds (about 10 seconds), each made of Turns for the Groups in the fight. Every Being belongs to a Group and acts on that Group’s Turn.

  • Turn Order: Heroes act first.
  • Surprise: If there’s a risk of surprise, Heroes roll Detect. Those who fail can’t act on the first Hero Turn.

Actions

You get one Action and a Move on your Turn.

  • Attack: Make an Attack Check with a Weapon; on a Pass, deal its damage.
  • Cast: Cast a Spell.
  • Hold: Save an Action or Move for an off-Turn moment.
  • Jump: Spend half your Speed to leap up to that distance horizontally.
  • Rush: Gain extra Speed this Turn equal to your Speed.
  • Use: Use or interact with an Item.

Move

Your Speed is how far you can Move on your Turn. You can split your movement around your Action however you like.

Flanking

If at least two Allied Beings are Close to a Foe (and the Foe is no more than one size larger), that Foe is Vulnerable.

Defending

You can Block or Dodge if you aren’t Incapacitated:

  • Block: Endure Save, Hindered vs Ranged Attacks.
  • Dodge: Reflex Save, Hindered if wearing Heavy Armor.
  • On a Pass, ignore one of the attack’s highest rolled damage dice.

Armor, Immune, Weak

  • Armor: Subtract from incoming Attack damage.
  • Immune: You take no damage of that type.
  • Weak: Ignores Armor and Immune, and rolls one extra damage die.

Focusing

Some effects require Focus to stay active. You can only Focus on one effect at a time; starting a new Focus ends the old one.

Dying

Beings die if any of the following are true:

  • Their HP is reduced to 0.
  • Their body is destroyed.
  • They reach 5 Fatigue.

Dead Beings can’t regain HP.

Zones

Each Enemy acts according to its assigned Zone:

  • Frontline: Moves Close and fights in Melee.
  • Midline: Moves Close to attack, then falls back to Near. At half HP or lower, it behaves like Backline.
  • Backline: Stays at Far, attacking with ranged or spells.

Casting Spells

To Cast, your hands must be free or holding a spellbook, staff, or trinket. When you Cast, decide:

  1. Are you dealing Damage, causing the Effect, or both?
  2. Who you Target and how the Spell is Delivered.
  3. How long it lasts (its Duration).
  • Damage only or Effect only: No Mana cost (1d6 damage if using the Damage Base).
  • Both Damage & Effect: Costs 1 Mana.

When to Roll Cast Checks

You only roll a Cast Check when an unwilling Being is a Target, or when keeping Focus on such a Being (see Duration).

Damage

Spells with a Damage Base deal 1d6 damage when cast for Damage. You can spend extra Mana to add +1d6 damage per Mana.

Effect

The Spell’s Effect text describes what happens to the Target, unless you cast it only to do damage.

Duration

  • By default, Effects last until the start of your next Turn.
  • You can Focus when you Cast to extend the Spell until you drop Focus.
  • If the Spell targets an unwilling Being and you want to keep Focus, you must spend 1 Mana and Pass a Cast Check each Round to maintain it.
  • Continual: Lasts until dismissed and doesn’t require Focus.

Delivery

Every Spell has a Delivery that defines how it reaches its Targets. Common types:

  • Aura: A radius around you affecting chosen Targets.
  • Cone: A short cone in front of you.
  • Cube / Sphere: A shaped Area in sight.
  • Line: A long strip of space (good for beams and blasts).
  • Remote: A bolt or effect centered directly on a Target.
  • Touch: A Close Target or yourself.
  • Imbue: A willing ally’s Weapon carries the Spell and can trigger it on a hit.
  • Glyph: A magic mark that later triggers the Spell in a small Area.

Time & Distance

Time

Term Approx. Time Example
Round ~10 seconds Move and take an Action.
Minute 6 Rounds A brief fight, pick a lock.
Scene ~10 minutes Search a room, a breather.
Hour 6 Scenes Research, downtime task.
Shift ~6 hours Travel, Craft, Rest.
Day 4 Shifts Recover from Fatigue.

Distance

Term From You
Close Within 5 ft.
Near 5–30 ft. away.
Far Beyond 30 ft., in sight.

Rest & Equipment

Rest

A Rest takes a Shift of light or no activity. If you’re fed and safe, you:

  • Restore HP, Luck, and Mana to their maximum.
  • If already at max HP, instead remove 1 Fatigue.

Breather

Once per Shift, you can eat a Ration and drink water to regain HP equal to your Might.

Inventory & Slots

  • Slots: You can carry (8 + Might) Item Slots.
  • If all Slots are full, you can’t Rush.
  • 0-Slot items: 1–10 of the item share 1 Slot.
  • 1 Slot: Carried in one hand.
  • 2 Slots: Requires two hands to carry.
  • 3 Slots: Large and heavy.

Coins don’t take Slots. Everyday clothing worn doesn’t take Slots (Armor isn’t clothing).

Armor (Quick)

  • Armor gives you an Armor Rating if it’s higher than your own.
  • If your Might is below the armor’s requirement, you’re Restrained while wearing it.
  • Equipping or removing Armor takes a Scene (~10 minutes).

Weapons (Quick)

Key Weapon properties and grips:

  • Brawl: Uses Brawl; can Grapple or Shove.
  • Brutal: Crits deal +1 damage die.
  • Cleave: Can hit two Targets for half damage each.
  • Entangle: Can Grapple.
  • Finesse: Can attack with Finesse.
  • Keen: Crits on Attack Checks 1 lower.
  • Long: +5 ft. to Range.
  • Near: Can only target up to Near.
  • Ranged: Uses Ranged; attacking a Close Target is Hindered.
  • Shield: Ignores Hinder when Blocking Ranged; can Shove.
  • Thrown: Can be thrown to Near, or Far with Hinder.
  • 1H: One-handed.
  • 2H: Two-handed.
  • F: Fist; hands count as free.
  • V: Versatile; 1H normally, higher damage die in 2H.

Dual-Wielding: If you have two weapons equipped, you can skip your Move to make an extra attack with your off-hand weapon.

Statuses

Common Status effects and what they mean in play:

  • Berserk: Can’t Focus; only Attacks, Moves, or Rushes. No Morale Checks.
  • Blinded: Can’t see; counts as Vulnerable.
  • Burning: Takes listed damage at the start of its Turn until the fire is doused.
  • Charmed: Can’t willingly attack the charmer.
  • Confused: Checks and Saves are Hindered; Saves against its actions have Favor.
  • Dazed: Can’t Focus or Move unless it spends an Action to do so.
  • Fatigued: Each Fatigue takes 1 Slot; at 3+ can’t Rush; at 5, dies.
  • Frightened: −2 penalty to damage dealt.
  • Incapacitated: Can’t Focus, act, or Move; automatically fails Might and Dexterity Checks; is Vulnerable.
  • Invisible: Can’t be seen by normal senses; creatures that can’t see it act as Blinded for Checks/Saves involving it.
  • Paralyzed: Incapacitated and Speed 0.
  • Prone: Must spend 10 ft. of Speed to stand; can only crawl (2 ft. of Speed for 1 ft. moved), can’t Rush, and is easier to hit in Melee.
  • Restrained: Vulnerable and Speed 0.
  • Sickened: −2 to healing received; extra rider effects may be listed.
  • Suffocating: While unable to breathe, gains Fatigue over time; Heroes roll d8 each Round vs Might, Enemies gain 1 Fatigue per Round.
  • Unconscious: Blinded, Incapacitated, and Prone; Close attacks against it always Crit.
  • Vulnerable: Its Attack Checks and Saves against Attacks are Hindered; Attacks against it have Favor.
